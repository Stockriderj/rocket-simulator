Preparation

In the first stage, drag the sliders to build your rocket. However, not all rockets are perfect. Luckily, I'll radio you information from the black panel underneath the Launch button. Be sure to follow my advice, or your space program will end badly!

Don't forget to also set the payload you'd like to carry. The number next to the name of the payload is its weight. Make sure to give your rocket enough thrust! This can be tricky, though, as you'll need half of that number as fuel. So if you launched a LEO Satellite (1000), you'd need 500 units of fuel. (Just so you know, LEO = Low Earth Orbit, MEO = Middle Earth Orbit, and HEO = High Earth Orbit.)

Mission Income

Every time you launch a successful mission, it will generate income over time. The number next to its name in the Payload Select menu is equal to income generated per second. However, the generated income will deteriorate over time and your spacecraft will eventually stop generating income.

Things that could go wrong with your rocket

Lots of things can go wrong with your rocket. This is a list of them.

  • No fuel: Seriously, how do you take off with no fuel?
  • Insufficient thrust: You don't have enough thrust to lift your rocket up the ground. The launch will be the most pathetic of its kind.
  • One solid rocket booster: This may seem kind of picky, but when you have one solid rocket booster, it's impossible to balance out the thrust. Basically, there's too much thrust on one side that isn't balanced out on the other. This means your rocket will take an unpleasant U-turn in the air and then faceplant in the ground with some big booms and an even bigger loss of money.
